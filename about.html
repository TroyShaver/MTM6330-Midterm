What Makes Supreme Unique?

The majority of Skate brands were founded in California with West Coast skaters in mind. In its early years, Supreme’s products were specifically designed with East Coast skaters in mind. Which is why Jebbia put such an emphasis on higher quality products; due to New York’s harsher seasons. The now “iconic” Supreme Box Logo t-shirt, originally released for opening day in 1994, continues to remain one of, if not the most, sought out item from the brand. Throughout the years, Supreme has become increasingly more difficult to buy due to all the additional hype that now surrounds the brand. Supreme now does 2 seasons per year, releasing products every Thursday throughout the season. Although nothing Supreme puts out will ever be classified as “limited”, products are produced in limited quantities so that they don't get stuck with stuff nobody wants.

Who Buys It?

Supreme is sought after millions. Not only are the designs unique and rare, they are also influenced heavily by artists of all sorts. Art recognizes art, this is why you will find countless celebrities wearing Supreme. If that is not enough, teenagers will wait outside of Supreme stores in New York, and even Japan. They will wait for hours in these trechorous lineups to get the latest apparel the brand has to offer. It's quality offers a timeless piece your friends are sure to love.

WHAT HAS SUPREME BECOME?

A Billion Dollar Empire

Supreme streetwear has garnered thousands upon thousands devoted fans wanting the new line the clothing brand has to offer. The first Supreme store opened in 1994, and only costs around 12,000$.Just a few years back it was shown that Supreme sold a 50 percent stake in its business to the Carlyle Group for a reported $500 million, and raises a number of questions. This proves the sheer power of Supreme, establishing itself as a billion dollar empire.
